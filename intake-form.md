# Intake form

Sent to the client 24-48 hours before the call. Estimated time to complete: 10 minutes.

The form is short on purpose. Forcing the client to surface their biggest workflow pain point in writing is often the most valuable part of the engagement - they often answer the audit themselves while filling it out.

---

## The form

### 1. About your business

- Business name and website
- What does your business do, in one sentence?
- How many people work on this (full-time, part-time, contractors)?
- Approximate annual revenue range (under $250K / $250K-1M / $1M-5M / over $5M)

### 2. Your current stack

List every tool you use regularly. Examples: Google Workspace, Notion, Asana, Trello, Slack, ClickUp, HubSpot, Shopify, Mailchimp, MailerLite, Klaviyo, Calendly, Zapier, Make, etc.

Be honest about what's actually used vs. what's signed up for. We're auditing reality, not the org chart.

### 3. Where your time goes

In a normal week, how many hours go to each of these:

- Customer-facing work (calls, meetings, delivery)
- Marketing and content (writing, posting, scheduling)
- Operations (project management, internal coordination, scheduling)
- Sales (pipeline, outreach, follow-ups)
- Admin (email triage, scheduling, expense tracking)
- Other

### 4. Your biggest workflow pain right now

In 2-3 sentences, what's the single biggest workflow pain in your business right now?

The thing that, if it were 50% faster or 90% reliable, would unlock something else.

### 5. AI experience so far

- Have you used Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot for business tasks? (Yes / No / Tried but didn't stick)
- Are any AI workflows running unattended (without a human starting them)? (Yes / No)
- What's your team's general comfort with new tools? (Early adopter / Cautious / Resistant)

### 6. What you want from this audit

In 2-3 sentences, what does a great outcome from this audit look like to you?

(Be specific. "Save time" doesn't help. "I want to stop manually pulling weekly client reports together every Friday" does.)

### 7. Out of scope

Is there anything you don't want me to look at? (Specific tools, processes, people, departments?)

This isn't about hiding things. It's about respecting the boundaries of what you actually want help with.

### 8. Anything else I should know before the call?

Open field. Anything from "we just hired our first marketer" to "I'm planning to fire our PM in 2 weeks" is useful context.

---

## What I do with the responses

I read everything before the call. Two specific things I look for:

**A discrepancy between sections 3 and 4.** When the time breakdown doesn't match the stated pain point, that's a real signal. Example: someone says marketing is killing them, but the time breakdown shows 60% of their week goes to admin and email triage. That tells me the actual leverage is in admin, not marketing. We talk about that on the call.

**A gap between sections 2 and 5.** When someone has a sophisticated stack but no unattended workflows, the audit recommendation often isn't "add more tools" - it's "let's connect what you already have so it runs without you."
